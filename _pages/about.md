I am a **Postdoctoral Researcher** at the **School of Mathematical Sciences, Peking University**.

My research lies at the intersection of **AI for Biology**, **applied probability**, **stochastic processes**, and **machine learning**. I focus on developing mathematical and computational methods for modeling, inferring, and predicting complex biological dynamics from high-dimensional biological data.

In particular, I am interested in **stochastic gene regulatory networks**, **biochemical reaction systems**, and **single-cell dynamical processes**. My work aims to bridge mechanistic stochastic models with modern AI methods, including **generative models**, **flow matching**, **optimal transport**, and **neural network–based inference**, to characterize cell-state transitions, gene expression variability, and perturbation responses.

Methodologically, I draw on tools from **probability theory**, **stochastic processes**, **dynamical systems**, **numerical computation**, and **deep generative modeling**. My recent research explores how AI-driven frameworks can be used to learn interpretable biological dynamics and support predictive modeling for single-cell systems.

My current research topics include:

- AI for Biology and single-cell dynamical modeling
- Stochastic gene regulatory networks and biochemical reaction systems
- Generative modeling, flow matching, and optimal transport for biological dynamics
- Single-cell perturbation response prediction and virtual cell modeling
- Neural network–based parameter inference for stochastic biological models
